## Reddit Post 1: Builder Story (r/freelance or r/smallbusiness)
**Title:** I got tired of sending quotes into a black hole, so I built a tool that tells you when clients open them

I’ve been freelancing for years, and the worst part isn’t the work. It’s the silence after sending a quote.

You do the call, write the estimate, send it, then wait. You don’t know if they opened it, compared vendors, or forgot.

So I built SentQuote. It’s simple:
1. Build a quote (line items, total, optional deposit) in about 60 seconds.
2. Send a link instead of a PDF.
3. See exactly when they open it and how many times they view it.
4. If they want to move forward, they can accept and pay a deposit right there.

No CRM, no proposal builder, no bloated workflow. Just send, track, get paid.

It’s free for 5 quotes/month. I’m looking for blunt feedback from people who deal with quote follow-up.

## Reddit Post 2: Value-First Insight (r/smallbusiness)
**Title:** The #1 reason I was losing jobs wasn’t price. It was follow-up timing.

I reviewed 6 months of sent quotes and noticed a pattern: the deals I closed had fast follow-up right after the client re-engaged.

Problem: with PDF quotes, I had no timing signal. I was either too early, too late, or guessing.

Switching to trackable quote links changed that. Seeing "viewed 3 times today" gave me a clear moment to reach out.

If your client is not responding to your quote, timing might be the issue more than pricing.

Happy to share my follow-up sequence if useful.
